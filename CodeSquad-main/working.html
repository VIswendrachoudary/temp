College life is often a period where students must balance academic responsibilities with newfound financial independence. For many, this marks the first time they are responsible for managing their own finances, including rent, utilities, food, and entertainment. Given these challenges, the need for a practical budgeting tool tailored to the unique needs of students becomes clear. Imagine a web page that not only helps students track their spending but also suggests nearby restaurants and shops that fit within their financial constraints. Such a web page would offer a user-friendly experience, integrating budgeting tools with personalized, location-based recommendations to ensure students can live affordably without sacrificing their lifestyle.

Budget Manager

At the core of this platform is the Budget Manager. Here, students would input their monthly income, which could come from various sources such as part-time jobs, scholarships, or allowances. The Budget Manager would prompt students to categorize their expenses, allowing them to allocate funds for essential categories like:

  • Housing and Utilities
  • Entertainment
